Assumptions

This model assumes a death rate of 2.5169%. This is based off of data from CDC that, as of March 13th, reported 1,629 cases and 41 deaths. Those of you visiting the CDC's site some time after March 13th will see different numbers. Is this the most accurrate death rate? Absolutely not. That comes in the months or years after a disease has run its course.

Another assumption is a sick time of 3 weeks (21 days). This is based off of Harvard Resource Center, who states It depends on how sick you get. Those with mild cases appear to recover within one to two weeks. With severe cases, recovery can take six weeks or more." They are compiling information from the CDC, WHO, and John Hopkins University COVID-19 Interactive Map.

Disease transmission here is done via checking proximity between squares, and then performing a probability check based off of the distance. Those probabilities for transmission go as follows: Directly adjacent (80%), two squares away (50%), three squares away (25%).

In this model, we also allow for an individual to become infected and still have a chance to wash their hands (therby becoming healthy) once again. We're assumming an infected period of five days before this individual falls ill. Each day the individual is infected, there is a probability that the person can become healthy. This probability can be adjusted in the Handwashing Efficacy slider.

About

Hello, I'm Ron, CTO at Outco. Outco is a Software Engineering Career Accelerator that guides software engineers of all seniority to earn great jobs. (<=almost verbatim from the landing page on our website) :)

During a Tech Level Up session on March 13th, I began to build this model in light of recent events. Much news has come out about this COVID-19 Curve and I wanted to explore the possibility of modeling what ifs.

What if we were to travel and go about our daily lives at our current pace?

What if we began to isolate and reduced our interaction by 50%? 75%?

Please have fun with this model. We could all use some of that. These are extraordinary times. Call or web conference your friends/families. Also, keep yourselves informed.
